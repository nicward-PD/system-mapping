Dorset Community Assets Network

Methodology, Dependencies, and Source Materials

This document outlines how the dependencies and relationships in the network diagram were identified, based on a synthesis of standard UK local government/healthcare operating models and specific documentation for the Dorset area.

1

Strategic Health & Statutory Partnerships

The core triangle between Dorset Council, NHS Dorset (ICB), and Our Dorset (ICS) is dictated by the Health and Care Act 2022, which formally established Integrated Care Systems across England.

The Dependency:

The law mandates that local authorities and the NHS must collaborate on a joint health and wellbeing strategy. Furthermore, statutory guidance requires the ICS to embed the VCSE (Voluntary, Community, and Social Enterprise) sector in its governance. This is why the Dorset VCSA has a direct, strategic connection to the ICS.

Source Materials:

  • Our Dorset (The local ICS) - How we work together
  • NHS England: VCSE sector in Integrated Care Systems
2

VCSE Infrastructure as "Connectors"

Organizations like Community Action Network (CAN), Dorset Community Action (DCA), and Help & Kindness are recognized as "infrastructure" bodies in Dorset. Their primary stated purpose is to act as a bridge.

The Dependency:

Statutory bodies often find it too administratively burdensome to commission or manage hundreds of tiny grassroots groups directly. Instead, they fund these infrastructure bodies to provide capacity building, distribute micro-grants, and act as a unified voice for the sector.

Source Materials:

  • Community Action Network (CAN) - Partnerships
  • Help & Kindness - Dorset Community Mapping
3

Devolved Power and Asset Transfers

The links showing "Asset Transfers" and "Thriving Communities" represent specific policy mechanisms used to share power locally.

The Dependency:

Dorset Council holds physical assets (buildings, land) and localized budgets. Through Community Asset Transfers (CAT), the council officially hands over the management (and power) of these assets to Town/Parish Councils or Village Halls/Community Trusts to ensure sustainable local control.

Source Materials:

  • Dorset Council - Community Asset Transfer Policy
  • Public Health Dorset - Thriving Communities
4

Grassroots Referrals (Social Prescribing)

The connections between GP surgeries/NHS and local groups (like Citizens Advice or Food Banks) represent the growing practice of Social Prescribing.

The Dependency:

Medical professionals increasingly recognize that housing, debt, and isolation cause poor health. Therefore, there are formal pathways (and funding streams) to refer patients away from strictly medical interventions and toward supportive community assets.

Source Materials:

  • National Academy for Social Prescribing
Note on usage: The network diagram and this accompanying document form a synthesized conceptual model designed for facilitation. While based on real frameworks and actual Dorset organizations, the on-the-ground reality is often a much denser web of informal relationships. The primary goal of these tools is to simplify structures so teams can easily spot "gatekeepers" and "connectors" during conversations about power-sharing.
